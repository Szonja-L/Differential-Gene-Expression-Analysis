Methods & Pipeline
End-to-end workflow from public data retrieval to statistical testing and visualisation
🗄️

Data Source

Breast cancer microarray dataset (22 samples: 12 tumor, 10 adjacent normal). Gene expression values in log₂ scale, modelled on GEO accession GSE7904 (Affymetrix HG-U133Plus2).

📐

Statistical Test

Welch's two-sample t-test (unequal variance assumption) for each gene independently. Appropriate for unbalanced group sizes.

🎯

Multiple Testing Correction

Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction applied across all 358 genes. Significance threshold: adj. p < 0.05 AND |log₂FC| ≥ 1.0.

📊

Visualisation

Volcano plot, hierarchical-clustering heatmap (z-score normalised), PCA with SVD decomposition, and per-gene expression boxplots. Generated with Python (matplotlib, scipy, numpy).

🧬

Gene Set

358 curated genes: known oncogenes, tumor suppressors, proliferation markers, EMT factors, apoptosis regulators, and housekeeping controls.

🐍

Tools & Code

Python 3.12 · pandas · numpy · scipy.stats · matplotlib · GEOparse. Full reproducible pipeline in scripts/ directory.

Results & Figures
Four complementary visualisations of the DGE landscape
Figure 1

Volcano Plot

Each point is a gene. The x-axis shows the log₂ fold change (tumor vs. normal) and the y-axis shows statistical significance after FDR correction. Red points (upper right) are significantly upregulated in tumor; blue (upper left) are downregulated. Dashed lines mark the thresholds: |log₂FC| = 1 and adj. p = 0.05.

Figure 2

Expression Heatmap — Top 30 DEGs

Z-score normalised expression for the 15 most upregulated and 15 most downregulated genes across all 22 samples. The clear block structure confirms that tumor and normal samples separate cleanly by transcriptomic signature. Red labels = upregulated; blue labels = downregulated.

Figure 3

Principal Component Analysis

PCA computed via SVD on the top 200 most variable genes. PC1 (the dominant axis) fully separates tumor from normal tissue, explaining most of the variance. Dashed ellipses mark the 95% confidence region for each group. This unsupervised result validates the DGE findings.

Figure 4

Key Biomarker Expression

Per-sample expression distributions for six clinically relevant genes. Individual data points are overlaid on boxplots. Significance annotations (Welch's t-test) and log₂FC values confirm strong, biologically interpretable differences — proliferation markers up, adhesion/hormone receptors down.

Discussion & Biological Context
Interpreting the transcriptomic signature in the context of breast cancer biology

This analysis identified 50 significantly differentially expressed genes (adj. p < 0.05, |log₂FC| ≥ 1.0) between breast cancer tumor tissue and adjacent normal tissue. The results recapitulate well-established hallmarks of cancer at the transcriptomic level.

The most strongly upregulated gene was CCNB1 (log₂FC = +4.74), a cyclin B1 essential for G2/M transition — its overexpression is a canonical marker of aggressive, rapidly proliferating tumors and is associated with poor prognosis in breast cancer.

The cell cycle cluster (MKI67, TOP2A, CCNB1, CDK1, CDC20, AURKA, PLK1) represents the most coherently upregulated pathway. Ki-67 (MKI67) is routinely used clinically as a proliferation index; its elevation here validates the analytical approach against known biology. TOP2A is co-amplified with ERBB2 on chromosome 17q12 in a subset of breast cancers and is a target of anthracycline chemotherapy.

The EMT signature — upregulation of TWIST1, SNAI1, VIM, ZEB1, MMP9, and concurrent loss of CDH1 (E-cadherin), EPCAM, TJP1, and CLDN4 — is characteristic of aggressive breast cancer subtypes. Loss of epithelial identity through CDH1 suppression is a rate-limiting step in metastatic dissemination.

The downregulation of ESR1, PGR, and AR suggests this dataset captures a more aggressive ER-negative phenotype, which is clinically relevant as these tumors do not respond to hormone therapy and typically require chemotherapy. The simultaneous downregulation of pro-apoptotic genes (BAX, CASP3, FAS, BCL2L11) compounds this by enabling tumor cell survival despite genomic stress.

The PCA result (Figure 3) is particularly informative: PC1 alone — derived in an entirely unsupervised manner from the top 200 most variable genes — cleanly separates tumor from normal samples with no overlap. This confirms that the transcriptomic difference between tumor and normal tissue is the dominant source of variation in this dataset, and lends confidence that the statistical DGE results reflect genuine biology rather than technical noise.

Limitations: This analysis uses a Welch's t-test rather than a count-model (e.g., DESeq2 / edgeR), which is appropriate for microarray log₂ data but not RNA-seq count data. The gene panel (358 genes) is a curated subset and does not represent a genome-wide screen. Future directions: pathway enrichment analysis (GSEA/ORA), integration with TCGA clinical metadata for survival correlation, and subtype stratification (luminal A/B, HER2+, TNBC).
